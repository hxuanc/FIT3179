UNICORN STARTUPS

According to Wikipedia, privately held startup companies that are valued at over US$1 billion are known as unicorns. The term unicorn was first coined in year 2013, uses the name of a mythical animal to represent the rareness of such success. How does a company achieves this milestone - people may wonder. In this visualization, let us explore the world of unicorns and analyze their success from different perspectives!

From 2011 to 2022, there are

1165 UNICORNS from 15 INDUSTRIES

across 6 REGIONS and 49 COUNTRIES

Unicorn Startups around the World

Where are the unicorns from? Over the years, there has been a substantial growth in the number of unicorn startup companies. From the map below, we can observe that the United States leads the race. China and India are the second and third countries with highest number of unicorns. Research shows that a lot of unicorns are from countries rich with growth-stage funding (Embroker Team, 2022).

Top 10 Countries with Most Unicorns

Let's dive further into the Top 10 Countries with most unicorns! From the diagram below, we can observe that a majority of the unicorns from these 10 countries are from the Fintech, Internet software and services, and E-commerce and direct-to-consumer industries.

Industries and their Total Valuation

Now, you may wonder which are the most successful - or most valuable unicorns. From the treemap, we can observe that Fintech leads the race with the highest total valuation among all industries! We can also observe that among the top 10 most valued unicorns, 4 of them are from the Fintech industry. However, the leading unicorn ByteDance, is an Artificial Intelligence company with a valuation of $140 billion.

Investors of Unicorns and their Investment Breakdown

Investors give support in paving the road to success for unicorns. Looking at the investment breakdown of the Top 10 Investors, we can see that majority of them invests in unicorns from United States, China and India - which verifies that growth-stage funding are very important. We can also observe that the investors have a preference to invest Artificial Intelligence, Fintech, E-commerce and direct-to-consumer and Cybersecurity industries.
